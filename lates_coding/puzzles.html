Station 1

You are at the first station.

On the table, you see a bunch of cards spread out.

Each card has a stage of the software development cycle written on it, but they’re all mixed up.

You need to put them in the correct order to obtain the first digit of CANCEL_CODE.

Below are the shuffled cards:

  • Testing
  • Planning
  • Deployment
  • Designing
  • Maintenance
  • Analysis
  • Development

Your task is simple:

Drag the cards into the correct order, from the first thing a project needs to the last thing in the Software Development Life Cycle.

Correct!!

First digit of CANCEL_CODE = Correct position of Deployment / Correct position of Designing


Correct! the first digit of CANCEL_CODE is 2.

Take the note of the first digit and move to the next station.

You are at the second station.

Here, the setup looks like a project board.

Five task cards are placed across the screen, but they are all in the wrong order.

Below, you can see their dependency rules written clearly

The tasks are:

  • T1: Final Integration Testing
  • T2: Update Documentation
  • T3: Code Review
  • T4: Fix Remaining Bugs
  • T5: Write Bug Fixes

And the dependencies are:

  • T1 depends on T2 and T4
  • T2 depends on T3
  • T4 depends on T3 and T5
  • T3 and T5 don’t depend on anything

The tasks are:

  • T1: Final Integration Testing
  • T2: Update Documentation
  • T3: Code Review
  • T4: Fix Remaining Bugs
  • T5: Write Bug Fixes

Your task:

Drag the cards into a sequence that follows all the dependency rules.

Correct!!

Second digit of CANCEL_CODE = Position of T1 in the order


Correct! the second digit of CANCEL_CODE is 5.

Take the note of the second digit and move to the next station.

You stop at the third station.

This station looks like a planning board used for workload scheduling.

In the centre of the screen, you see the details for a single task:

Task details:

  • Task: Task T1 – Final Integration Testing
  • Effort required: 6 person-weeks

Assigned team members:

  • Ahmed – full-time (1 person-week per week)
  • Lisa – part-time, mornings only (0.5 person-weeks per week)

Only Ahmed and Lisa have the skills to complete this task, and only they will work on it until it is finished.

Your task:

Work out how many full weeks are required to complete 6 person-weeks of work together.


Correct! The task will take 4 weeks.

Third digit of CANCEL_CODE = Number of weeks needed to complete T1 , which is 4

You take the digit and move on to the next station.

You arrive at the fourth station.

This station looks like a developer’s desk setup. A function is displayed on the screen: 

    def score_bonus(points):
        if points >= 50:
            bonus = points * 2
        else:
            bonus = points + 10
        return bonus

Below the function, a note says:

“Run this function with the test value shown at the station.”

Test with: 35

Time is ticking...

Your task:

Figure out what the function returns when you call: score_bonus(35)


Correct! The return value is 45.

Fourth digit of CANCEL_CODE = Result of score_bonus(35) ÷ 5


Correct! the fourth digit of CANCEL_CODE is 9.

You take the fourth digit and move toward the final station.

You reach the final station.

This setup looks like a project planning table. Several risk cards are laid out in front of you.

Each one describes something that could cause trouble during a project.

Below are the risks:

  • A senior developer may be unavailable at a critical moment.
  • The team is using a technology they have never worked with before.
  • The client might change major requirements during the project.

Your task:

 Arrange the risks from most serious → least serious.

Correct!

Final digit of CANCEL_CODE = 4

A locked door with a keypad waits in front of you.

This is where you need to enter the complete CANCEL_CODE to stop the logic bomb.
